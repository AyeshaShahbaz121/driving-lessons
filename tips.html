Driving Safety Tips

Master the art of safe driving with expert advice and actionable insights.

Avoid Distractions

Definition: Distracted driving occurs when attention shifts from the road to tasks like texting or eating.

How to Avoid: Use hands-free devices and eliminate non-essential distractions while driving.

What to Do: If distracted, pull over safely and regain focus before resuming.

Brake Failure

Definition: When the braking system fails to stop the vehicle effectively.

How to Avoid: Maintain your brakes with regular servicing and fluid checks.

What to Do:

  • Shift to a lower gear to reduce speed.
  • Apply the emergency brake carefully.
  • Steer to a safe area and call for assistance.

Defensive Driving

Definition: Anticipating potential hazards to avoid accidents.

How to Avoid Risks: Maintain safe distances and avoid aggressive driving behaviors.

What to Do: Stay alert and proactively scan for potential risks on the road.

Weather-Specific Advice

Definition: Adjusting your driving to suit adverse weather conditions like rain or fog.

How to Avoid Risks:

  • Reduce speed and use headlights.
  • Ensure windshield wipers and tires are functional.

What to Do: If visibility is low, pull over to a safe location and wait until conditions improve.

Handling Tire Blowouts

Definition: Sudden tire failure causing potential loss of control.

How to Avoid: Regularly inspect tires and maintain proper inflation.

What to Do:

  • Firmly hold the steering wheel.
  • Gradually ease off the accelerator.
  • Pull over safely and replace the tire or call for help.
